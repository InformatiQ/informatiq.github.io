A few weeks in my new job in Paris after just moving in from Helsinki, my manager tells me in our biweekly 1 on 1 meeting

_"You have an issue with the team, nobody seems to want to work with you"_ +
Heart beats accelerate, blood rushes through my face and the urge respond violently to such a claim escalates. +
A deep breath and a few seconds later i respond supercially calmly +
_"could you give me more details, more facts or anything to helpme understand why"_

[TIP]
*First rule when dealing with negative feedback, always take a deep breath before reacting.*

At that moment all sorts of thoughts are rushing in my mind, I was not even fully attentive to my managers explanations. So again I take a deep breath and refocus on understanding the "WHY" of the feedback.+
Regardless of the details of the discussion that followed, the conclusion was: My manager got this sentiment from each and everyone and he does not have more details. I am all alone in this.

I have (or at least i thought so) 2 ways of dealing with this.

- *Engage my child* refuse to accept that feedback, retaliate and give negative feedback on each one of them. +
- *Engage my adult* try to understand WHY and HOW to remedy that.

I decided to act like an adult.

I reserved a 30 minutes slot for each team member (5 excluding my manager) and met with each of them with only 1 question to ask

 Why do you feel that you don't want to work with me?

And the intention of establishing a trust relationship.

After the round of 1on1 meetings, I came to a few reasons

* I do act like a jerk sometimes, specially that i have not worked in a team for a long time.
* I act in a different way than they are used to
* They don't really know me

The reason that stoodout was about me acting in a different way that they are used to. For example when I approach someone for a question, I don't start by saying hi or anysort of intro, but i do go straight to the question. Another example I don't say good morning to everyone when I arrive in the morning.

These some simple examples but there were other things and they all boil down to lack of communication. +
I do say hi when i arrive but i just throw it to the group (UDP way), I prefer to be direct when i talk work. That's just how I am and how i learned to act while living in finland for the past 8 years.
