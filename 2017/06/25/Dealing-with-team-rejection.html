Dealing with team rejection

TLDR

Team needs to establish healthy relationships between its members based on trust and communications.

A few weeks in my new job in Paris after just moving in from Helsinki, my manager tells me in our biweekly 1 on 1 meeting

"You have an issue with the team, nobody seems to want to work with you"
Heart beats accelerate, blood rushes through my face and the urge respond violently to such a claim escalates.
A deep breath and a few seconds later i respond supercially calmly
"could you give me more details, more facts or anything to helpme understand why"

First rule when dealing with negative feedback, always take a deep breath before reacting.

At that moment all sorts of thoughts are rushing in my mind, I was not even fully attentive to my managers explanations. So again I take a deep breath and refocus on understanding the "WHY" of the feedback.+ Regardless of the details of the discussion that followed, the conclusion was: My manager got this sentiment from each and everyone and he does not have more details. I am all alone in this.

I have (or at least i thought so) 2 ways of dealing with this.

  • Engage my child refuse to accept that feedback, retaliate and give negative feedback on each one of them.

  • Engage my adult try to understand WHY and HOW to remedy that.

I decided to act like an adult.

I reserved a 30 minutes slot for each team member (5 excluding my manager) and met with each of them with only 1 question to ask

  • Why do you feel that you don’t want to work with me?

And the intention of establishing a trust relationship. Ready to admit my mistakes and resolve any issues.

After the round of 1on1 meetings, I came to a few reasons

  • I do act like a jerk sometimes, specially that I have not worked in a team for a long time.

  • I act in a different way than they are used to

  • They don’t really know me

The reason that stood out was about me acting in a different way that they are used to. For example when I approach someone for a question, I don’t start by saying hi or anysort of intro, but i do go straight to the question. Another example I don’t say good morning to everyone when I arrive in the morning.
I do say hi when i arrive but i just throw it to the group (UDP way), I prefer to be direct when i talk work. That’s just how I am and how i learned to act while living in finland for the past 8 years.

These some simple examples among other things and they all boil down to lack of communication and cultural difference.

Now that the WHY is known and communication established with the team, the rest is easy. Well relatively easy and requires vigilance and hard work.
Relationships are not made by meeting once, I had taken the task of making sure I communicate with everyone in the team make sure all potential issues are resolved early and continuous two-way feedback. Yes I also had to work on my habits and flaws.

IMHO I don’t think I would have resolved the issue by only working on my attitude without openly communicating with my team. I beleive a big part was not about my attitude. How many times have you had a colleague who had annoying traits but yet it did not affect your work relations.

A team is not afraid of conflicts. The team uses trust and communication to deal with conflicts
